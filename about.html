About SIMRIX

Engineering simulation tools built with clarity, control, and long-term reliability in mind.

Origin & Motivation

SIMRIX was created to address a common challenge in engineering workflows: the need for simulation tools that are both powerful and transparent. Many modern tools prioritise automation and abstraction, often at the cost of clarity and interpretability.

The objective behind SIMRIX is simple — to provide focused, engineer-controlled software that enables understanding, not just output generation.

What SIMRIX Is

  • A growing collection of domain-specific engineering tools.
  • Designed for controlled analysis and reproducibility.
  • Built with long-term version stability in mind.
  • Focused on clarity over automation.

What SIMRIX Is Not

  • Not a black-box optimisation engine.
  • Not a marketing-driven SaaS platform.
  • Not a replacement for fundamental engineering reasoning.

Engineering Principles

The following principles guide the design and development of all SIMRIX tools.

  • Transparent Models

    Underlying models and assumptions are documented, explicit, and engineer-auditable.

  • Version Permanence

    All released versions remain accessible to ensure reproducibility and validation.

  • Controlled Inputs

    Users maintain meaningful control over parameters and assumptions.

  • Responsible Distribution

    System-bound licensing ensures appropriate usage while maintaining accessibility.

  • Practical Application

    Tools are built for real engineering workflows, not purely theoretical demonstration.

  • Long-Term Stability

    Development prioritises continuity and maintainability over rapid feature churn.

Intended Use

SIMRIX tools are intended for engineers, researchers, and technically inclined users who value understanding and traceability in computational workflows.

The software supports controlled experimentation, comparative analysis, and structured engineering studies.

Future Direction

SIMRIX is designed as a growing ecosystem rather than a single application. New tools will continue to follow the same engineering philosophy and structural consistency.

The long-term objective is to build a stable, modular collection of engineering utilities that remain reliable and accessible over time.

Commitment

SIMRIX is built with a commitment to technical integrity, reproducibility, and responsible distribution. The goal is not rapid expansion, but sustainable development aligned with sound engineering practice.
